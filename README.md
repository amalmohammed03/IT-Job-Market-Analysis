 🚀 IT Job Market Analysis (2025–2026)

 📌 Project Overview
This project performs an end-to-end analysis of the AI & IT job market using real-world data. The goal is to understand how factors such as job demand, experience level, and location influence salary trends across various technology roles.

The analysis focuses on extracting meaningful insights from job postings and presenting them through structured analysis and visualizations.

---

 🛠 Tech Stack
- **Python**
- **Pandas** – Data manipulation & analysis  
- **Seaborn & Matplotlib** – Data visualization  

---

 📂 Dataset
- AI Jobs Market Dataset (2025–2026)
- Contains job roles, salaries, demand scores, experience levels, and country data

---

 ⚙️ Data Processing
- Cleaned column names for consistency  
- Removed non-geographical entries (e.g., "Global")  
- Created a new feature: **Average Salary**  
- Prepared dataset for analysis and visualization  

---

 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

 1️⃣ Job Role Demand Analysis
- Identified the most in-demand roles in the market  
- Top roles include:
  - LLM Engineer  
  - Robotics Engineer  
  - Prompt Engineer  
  - Generative AI Engineer  

👉 Insight: Emerging AI roles dominate demand trends.

---

 2️⃣ Experience vs Salary
- Compared salaries across experience levels:
  - Entry (0–2 yrs)  
  - Mid (3–5 yrs)  
  - Senior (6–9 yrs)  
  - Lead (10+ yrs)  

👉 Insight: Salary variation across experience levels is relatively small, indicating skill-based compensation.

---

 3️⃣ Country-wise Salary Analysis
- Analyzed top countries by average salary  
- Highest-paying regions include:
  - France  
  - Netherlands  
  - UAE  
  - Germany  

👉 Insight: European regions offer slightly higher compensation for AI roles.

---

 4️⃣ Demand vs Salary (Key Analysis 🔥)
- Visualized relationship between demand score and salary  
- Added job category for better interpretation  

👉 Insight:
- Strong positive correlation between demand and salary  
- High-demand roles cluster in high-salary range  
- AI/ML roles dominate the premium segment  

---

 📈 Key Insights

- 📌 High-demand roles offer higher salaries  
- 📌 AI and ML roles dominate both demand and compensation  
- 📌 Salary differences across experience levels are minimal  
- 📌 Location influences salary, but not drastically  
- 📌 Skill specialization is more important than years of experience  

---

 📊 Visualizations Included

- Top Job Roles Demand

---

 🚀 Conclusion

The analysis highlights that job demand plays a key role in determining salary, with high-demand roles clustering in the higher salary range, particularly in AI and Machine Learning domains.
